The Murder at the Vicarage is a work of detective fiction by Agatha Christie and first published in the UK.
It features Miss Marple in her first appearance in a full-length novel.
The story unfolds in the quaint village of St. Mary Mead, where Colonel Protheroe is found dead in the vicar's study.
Miss Marple uses her keen observation skills and knowledge of human nature to solve the mystery.
The narrative is filled with red herrings, clever plot twists, and a cast of suspicious characters.
Christie's mastery of the cozy mystery genre is evident in every page.
